How to get there

Part of Crete's charm is its oceanic border.

However, this can make the island a hard to get to. The best means of travel to Crete is via airplane or boat from the mainland. For air travel, there are two main airports: Herklion and Chania International.


Weather

  • Crete has a Mediterranean climate with mild, rainy winters and hot, dry summers.
  • During the summer, the average temperature falls between 24-26°C.
  • During the winter, the average temperature falls between 11-17°C.

Top 5 places to visit

  1. Heraklion Archaeological Museum
  2. Knossos Palace
  3. Cave of Diktaion Andron
  4. Vai Beach
  5. Old Venetian Port of Chania

Food recommondations

The Greek islands as well as the Greek mainland are famed for their beautifully rich cuisine. Above all, Cretans are also renowned for their cheeses. In fact, many towns even have their own unique signature cheese. Make sure to try:

  1. Cretan Cheeses
  2. Sarikopitakia (Cheese Pie)
  3. Chochlioi boubouristi (Fried Snails)
  4. Dakos
  5. Raki or Tsikoudia (Cretan Brandy)

Useful phrases

The official language is Crete is Greek. Due to its ancient origins, many English speakers struggle to pronounce Greek words. Luckily, there are many online resources to assist you with correct Greek pronounciation, and many Cretan people have some grasp of English if you are really stuck. Here are some key prases to get you started:

  • Hello/Goodbye! - Giásas! (informal: giásou)
  • Thank you - Efharistó
  • Yes - Ne
  • No - Óhi
  • Please - Parakaló
  • Do you speak English? - Xérete angliká?
